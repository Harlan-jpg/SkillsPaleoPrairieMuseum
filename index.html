PaleoPrairie Museum

Who We Are

Our museum began with a simple but powerful idea: to keep the fossils of western Kansas in western Kansas. Nestled in a region rich with prehistoric history, we are committed to uncovering, preserving, and showcasing the ancient life that once roamed our land. It all started when a local dig site uncovered a nearly complete fossilized skeleton of a marine reptile, and rather than see it shipped out of state, a small team of paleontology enthusiasts and community members worked together to find a way to preserve it locally. From that effort grew a mission to support fossil digs in western Kansas and build a place where discoveries stay rooted in the land they were found. Today, our museum proudly funds archaeological digs, preserves rare fossils, and tells the story of Kansas’ ancient life—from the inland sea to the towering dinosaurs that left their mark here millions of years ago.

What Is Paleontology?

Paleontology is the study of the history of pre-historic life on Earth based off of fossils found in the ground. Fossils are the remains of plants, animals, diffent types of fungi, bacteria, and other single-celled organisms that have been replaced by rocky material or impressions made in the stone by organisms preserved in rock. Talk about getting stuck between a rock and a hard place!
